Use this list to quickly open a browser tab, streaming your favorite station from an internet connected software defined radio receiver (SDR). If signals are weak or noisy, try another station or switch to a different receiver location. You can tune around and find more stations on any of the SDRs. They not only receive AM / mediumwave, but also work fabulously on shortwave.

No direct studio streams; no geoblocking by the broadcasters!

How to use:
  1. Click on a city or region.
  2. Click the button for a station.
  3. Click on a receiver location to listen.
  4. If necessary, click to start the audio.

Night is best for mediumwave, and it is always night time somewhere. If you are not sure if it is day or night in the place you want to listen from, have a look at a day and night map to see where the light and dark areas are.

Reception range is limited during local daylight hours due to ionospheric absorbtion; night time range is very long - at least hundreds and sometimes a thousand kilometers or more. WebSDRs can accommodate plenty of users and site operators typically do not limit your time on the server. KiwiSDRs have excellent features, such as AM stereo modes and synchronous demodulation. Have fun and enjoy the ease of AM / mediumwave listening on internet SDRs!

Please be a fair listener on the KiwiSDR radio servers. They are better radios but can only accommodate a few listeners at once. Avoid excessive time or opening multiple tabs from the same site at once, as site operators detect and block users or frequencies drawing too much bandwidth.

SDR sites are operated by volunteer radio enthusiasts. Some of them will accept donations from listeners, so consider helping to support the sites if you find them to be useful.

As new radios go online and others drop off, this application will be updated periodically. Check for updates!
